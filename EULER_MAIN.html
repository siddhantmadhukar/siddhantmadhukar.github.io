Euler Motors

I joined Euler Motors in 2022 as a Battery R&D Engineer I, where I initially focused on developing 3-wheeler lithium-ion battery packs in the 10–15 kWh range. Over time, I transitioned to working on 4-wheeler high-voltage battery systems, taking end-to-end responsibility for the project and driving innovations in weight optimization and cooling strategies.

Euler Projects Timeline

  1. 2024–2025

    Design Release for 4W Storm Battery

    Production Battery Design for 4W Storm — Led the final production design release of the battery module in 30 kWh and 19 kWh configurations. Owned integration of a flexible printed-circuit (FPC) PCB into the injection-molded Cell Contact System (CCS), engineering snap-fit busbar features so the CCS doubles as a precision carrier/fixture for automated laser welding. The design improved busbar positional stability and planarity, reduced separate fixturing, and increased assembly repeatability for series production.

  2. 2025

    Battery Design & Prototyping for 4W Storm

    I worked on the design of the battery box for the 4W Storm pickup truck, including the high-voltage routing for a battery pack operating above 300 V. To address the challenges of multiple modules, we implemented flexible busbar joints, improving reliability and ease of assembly. I also optimized the busbar cross-sectional area by differentiating between fast-charging and non-fast-charging modules, which reduced copper usage and delivered significant cost savings while maintaining performance and safety standards.

  3. 2024

    Serpentine Cooling Channel Development

    Initiated the transition toward hermetic sealing by developing battery enclosure designs using fully formed deep-drawn parts. This approach reduced the number of weld seams to localized spot welds, significantly lowering leakage risk and improving manufacturability. I carried out multiple surfacing iterations to ensure design-for-manufacturing (DFM) compliance, while strategically adding ribs and gussets during forming to achieve localized strength without increasing material thickness. This not only enhanced structural integrity but also contributed to substantial reductions in both cost and weight.

  4. 2023–2024

    Prismatic Cell Module Design & Manufacturing

    Designed a production-ready prismatic-cell module with a robust compression system to extend cycle life. Evaluated manufacturing routes—including high-pressure die casting—to achieve stiff, durable compression plates. Developed custom compression pads to meet defined pressure and thickness targets, while managing tight tolerances to enable reliable automated laser welding. Conducted modal analysis in Altair HyperMesh/OptiStruct to validate structural integrity and mitigate resonances. Executed SAE J2380 vibration qualification (~38 hours cumulative exposure) simulating ~120k-mile road use, overseeing fixturing and post-test analysis to eliminate resonance risks.

  5. 2023–2024

    Forming & Surfacing for Hermetic Enclosures

    Initiated the transition toward hermetic sealing by developing battery enclosure designs using fully formed deep-drawn parts. Reduced weld seams to localized spot welds, lowering leakage risks and improving manufacturability. Multiple surfacing iterations ensured DFM compliance, with ribs and gussets strategically added to achieve localized strength without extra thickness. This approach improved structural integrity while reducing cost and weight.

  6. 2022–2023

    Prototype Development with Prismatic LFP Cells

    Transitioned from NMC cylindrical cells to prismatic LFP cells to lower costs and reduce components. Designed cooling channels and module architecture for the first prototype with this chemistry, ensuring efficient thermal management. This laid the foundation for a cost-effective and scalable battery system with improved reliability.
